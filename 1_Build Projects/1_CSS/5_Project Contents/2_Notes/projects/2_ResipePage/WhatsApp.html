Got it Let’s make *Evolution of Software Architecture* very clear and simple for you, step by step: --- ## Evolution of Software Architecture ### 1. *Monolithic Architecture (1960s–70s)*
  • Everything (UI + Logic + Data) in *one single program*.
  • Hard to maintain and scale.
 Example: Old payroll or calculator software. --- ### 2. *Structured / Modular Architecture (1970s–80s)*
  • Code divided into *modules & functions*.
  • Better organization, easier debugging.
 Example: Banking apps with separate functions for deposit, withdraw, balance. --- ### 3. *Client–Server Architecture (1980s–90s)*
  • Application split into *Client (UI)* and *Server (data, logic)*.
  • Popular when networking grew.
